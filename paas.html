Platform as a Service (PaaS)

  • by:Kelly Bamfo
  • date:May 10th, 2021

Cloud computing is the distribution of computer resources such as servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence over the Internet. Cloud computing will allow companies and individuals to utilize only the resources they need without monitoring the physical datacenters provided by the could vendor(s). Therefore, Cloud computing saves its users money and time. Well-known Cloud Computing vendors are Amazon Web Service, Microsoft Azure, Oracle, Salesforce, SAP, IBM Cloud, Google Cloud Platform, and VMWare.

The Cloud Computing stack is composed of three main models: Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Software as a Service (SaaS). IaaS deals with the more complex technologies in the cloud computing stack, such as networking, storage, servers, and virtualization. PaaS is the model stacked on-top of the IaaS; therefore, it utilizes the IaaS functionalities. The technologies used in the PaaS model are Operating System, middleware, runtime. Finally, the least complex part of the stack is the SaaS; this is the interface that users will interact with. Examples of SaaS are youtube.com, google.com, alfredstate.edu. The technologies at this level are the Application and the Data. Throughout the rest of this paper, we will focus on PaaS. PaaS helps developers focus on deploying and maintaining applications without the need to manage the IaaS. Once a developer deploys their application on a PaaS, they can access that application immediately. PaaS makes it easy and fast for developers to create and delete proofs-of-concept; it helps them save money because the proof-of-concept application will charge developers for how long it runs. Additionally, the cost of benefits, the developer will not have to pay for an IT admin or the physical systems they are utilizing. PaaS platforms consistently update their service by adding more tools such as the DevOps tools, collaboration tools, and API marketplaces. The disadvantages of using PaaS are a lack of control over the resources being used and vendor lock-in; this makes it hard for developers to migrate their application from one vendor to another.

PaaS heavily relates to the web development field. When a web developer finishes an application, they can host it on a PaaS platform such as Heroku or AWS Beanstalk. They can have users and investors test out their applications before deploying the application to the masses. Before this research, I never knew that the PaaS was the level where most developers work. I always thought that the development teams worked at the SaaS level. Now I know that the SaaS model is distributed to the public for usage and does not require much technical knowledge to operate.
